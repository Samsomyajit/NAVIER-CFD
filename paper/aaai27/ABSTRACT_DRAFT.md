# Abstract draft

Scientific machine-learning ecosystems provide increasingly capable neural operators, simulation datasets, and tool-using language-model agents, yet the decisions connecting these resources are often weakly governed and difficult to reproduce. We introduce **NAVIER-CFD**, a CFD-first framework that represents scientific objectives as persistent research contracts, filters models through hard task compatibility, ranks compatible candidates using task-similarity-weighted evidence, exposes deterministic scientific tools through a controlled interface, and records approvals, resource usage, findings, and stopping decisions. These results support contract-governed scientific agents as a complementary layer above model libraries and simulation benchmarks, improving the traceability and reliability of CFD model selection without treating language-model reasoning as numerical evidence.
